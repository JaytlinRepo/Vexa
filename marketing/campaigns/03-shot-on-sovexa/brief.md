# Campaign 03 — Product-as-hero

## The big idea
Apple's "Shot on iPhone" campaign treats the iPhone like a sculpture: lit, composed, alone in space. Sovexa's product is software, but the principle holds — strip away every UI element except one, float it on ivory, treat it like an art object.

The viewer doesn't read a screenshot. They look at a *thing*.

## Composition rules (template C — The Product)
- Pure ivory canvas
- ONE in-app card or chart, perfectly composed, hovering in negative space
- Soft long-shadow underneath: `0 60px 120px -40px rgba(20,16,10,.20)`
- ≥40% empty above the object, ≥30% empty below
- Caption at the very bottom: ONE line, ink-secondary, 28px sans-serif. ≤7 words.
- Tiny SOVEXA mono mark, bottom-right or centered

## What to feature (one per ad)

### A. The trend chart
The amber forecast curve from HQ v3, isolated. Caption: *"Maya saw it first."*

### B. The hook card
The three-hook stack with #2 highlighted in amber. Caption: *"Alex picked the one."*

### C. The shot list
Riley's numbered shot list, clean. Caption: *"Riley wrote the open."*

### D. The approve button
A single hero rendering of the Approve button — cursor mid-tap, soft ripple. Caption: *"That was the meeting."*

## What we don't do
- No Macbook frames, no iPhone frames around the screenshot
- No callout arrows, no annotations
- No "before / after" comparisons
- No dashboard chrome — extract just the one card
