# Campaign 04 — *Maya sees it first.*

## Why this is the lead campaign
Maya's analysis is one of the two biggest draws to Sovexa. The differentiator: she pulls from seven sources (Google Trends, NewsAPI, YouTube, Reddit, RSS, plus IG and TikTok engagement data), runs Holt smoothing for forecasts, and correlates trend spikes against the user's own past post performance. That's not a chatbot writing copy. That's a real analytics engine.

The campaign showcases the **product UI** — the warm-ivory forecast chart, the spike detector, the trend report card — as the hero. Type takes a supporting role.

## The line
> **Maya sees it first.**

Variants for different formats:
- *"4 a.m. — Maya sees it."* (story, reel)
- *"Seven sources. One report. Yours."* (carousel)
- *"The trend, before it's a trend."* (billboard / hero)

## Card variants

### A — The Forecast (square 1080×1080)
Apple-tier product-as-hero. The HQ v3 forecast card hovering on ivory with a soft long-shadow. The amber projected curve is the focal element. Caption beneath: *"Maya sees it first."* No chrome, no callouts.

### B — The Source List (portrait 1080×1350)
Product-forward. A clean editorial list of the seven data sources Maya watches, each with a tiny live-pulse dot. Caption: *"While you sleep."*

### C — The Receipt (square 1080×1080)
A single trend-report card showing: spike %, the correlated past post, the recommended angle. Like a museum-card label for one beautiful object. Caption: *"4 a.m."*

### D — The Number (square 1080×1080)
Pure stat treatment. *7* in massive Cormorant amber. Mono caption: `DATA SOURCES SHE WATCHES.` That's it.

## What we are *not* doing
- Showing Maya as a person/avatar — she's a role, not a character; we let the work represent her.
- Annotating the UI with arrows or labels — Apple never does, neither do we.
- Calling her "an AI." She's the analyst.
