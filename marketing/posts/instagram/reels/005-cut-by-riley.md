# Reel 005 — *Cut by Riley.* (the before/after reveal)

**This is the highest-leverage marketing asset for Sovexa.** Show, don't tell. Raw shaky 90 seconds becomes a paced 30-second vertical edit. That transformation *is* the pitch.

**Reference:** Apple "Shot on iPhone" reveals. Tesla's no-VO product demos. Old Nike "Just Do It" tempo cuts.

**Format:** Native vertical 1080×1920. No talking head. No voiceover. Music does everything.
**Music:** Cinematic build — sparse intro, kick on the third beat, tight loop on the cuts. Reference: Bonobo, Ólafur Arnalds, or licensed cinematic-electronic.
**Color grade:** Raw is left honest (slightly muddy phone footage). Polished is graded warm-ivory matching brand.

---

## Storyboard (18 seconds)

### 00:00 — 00:01 · Title card
Pure ivory frame. Cormorant italic, ink, centered:
> *You record.*
Music: single piano note. Hold.

### 00:01 — 00:06 · The raw
Cut to phone-shot footage. Real, unfiltered, slightly shaky. **5 seconds of B-roll** of the user doing whatever they do — making coffee, walking to a desk, talking off-camera.
Top-left of frame, in tiny mono: `RAW · 1:32 · YOUR PHONE`
Music: ambient drift, no kick yet.

### 00:06 — 00:07 · The pivot
Frame freezes. Audio cuts.
On-screen card: italic amber:
> *He directs.*

### 00:07 — 00:14 · The output
Music kicks in — first hit. Beat-synced cuts begin. **Seven hard cuts in seven seconds** — same footage, but Riley's reorganization:
1. Tight close-up open (no audio for 1s, just visual)
2. Cut on beat → wide
3. Cut on beat → action moment
4. Cut on beat → speech beat
5. Cut on beat → b-roll
6. Cut on beat → reaction
7. Cut on beat → close shot, hold the eyes

Lower-third throughout: amber italic credit, micro: `CUT BY RILEY`
Top-right corner micro: `0:30 · READY TO POST`

### 00:14 — 00:17 · The receipt
Cut to ivory. Editorial summary card slides up:
- `BEATS DETECTED · 6`
- `CUTS · 7`
- `RUNTIME · 0:30`
- `EXPORT · INSTAGRAM REEL · 9:16`
Music holds, sustained.

### 00:17 — 00:18 · The signature
Cut to ivory. Cormorant italic:
> *Cut by Riley.*
Beneath, mono: `SOVEXA.AI`
Music exits clean.

## Why this works
1. **No voice.** The transformation speaks. Apple's "Shot on iPhone" never explains what's happening.
2. **The raw is honest.** Showing real shaky phone footage makes the polish feel earned.
3. **Beat-sync sells the technology** without naming it. The viewer feels "this thing knows when to cut."
4. **The receipt at the end** quietly shows the *system* — beats, cuts, runtime, export. It tells the founder/operator brain: this is real software, not a filter.

- **Niche-specific cuts:** record one of these for fitness, food, finance, lifestyle, coaching. Same structure. Different B-roll.
