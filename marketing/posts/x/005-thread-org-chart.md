# Thread: "I built an org chart for my content."

**1/**
I used to be a one-person content team. Strategist, copywriter, editor, analyst. Me, me, me, me.

Last week I made an org chart. I'm the CEO now. Here's everyone who works for me.

**2/ Maya — Trend & Insights**
She watches Google Trends, NewsAPI, YouTube, Reddit, and three RSS feeds. Files a report every Monday. Flags spikes the second they happen.

I haven't opened a trend tool in 14 days.

**3/ Jordan — Content Strategist**
Sunday 6pm she drops next week's calendar. Pillars, posting times, angles, what each piece is supposed to do. I approve it Monday morning over coffee.

**4/ Alex — Copywriter**
Once Jordan's plan is locked, Alex writes hooks. Three to five per piece. He flags the one that'll stop a thumb. I approve. Done.

**5/ Riley — Creative Director**
Once the copy ships, Riley writes the shot list. Pacing, scene cuts, what beat the cut needs to land on. He hands it to me with editing notes.

**6/**
None of them ask me to write a prompt. None of them sit in a chat window. They just *do their jobs* and hand me the output with three buttons:

Approve. Reject. Reconsider.

**7/**
I'm not using an AI tool. I'm running a company.
