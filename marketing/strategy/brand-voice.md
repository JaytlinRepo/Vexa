# Brand Voice

## One-line positioning
> Sovexa gives you a four-person AI content team. You run it like a CEO.

## Voice attributes
- **Editorial, not enthusiastic.** We write like a magazine, not a launch tweet.
- **Quiet confidence.** Declarative sentences. No hype words.
- **Concrete over abstract.** Numbers, names, decisions — not "10x your content."
- **Short.** A line that lands beats a paragraph that explains.

## Lexicon

### Use
- *team, employees, hires, staff*
- *Maya, Jordan, Alex, Riley* (use their names)
- *brief, plan, hook, shot list, output, decision*
- *approve, reject, reconsider* (mirrors product buttons)
- *meeting, calendar, task, pipeline*
- *creator, founder, operator, CEO*
- *trend, signal, spike, gap*

### Avoid
- *AI tool, AI assistant, chatbot, prompt, LLM*
- *unleash, supercharge, 10x, game-changer, revolutionary*
- *seamless, robust, leverage, synergy*
- *"Just type a prompt and..."* (we are anti-prompt)
- Anything ending in "-er-of-your-dreams"

## Sentence shapes that work for us
- "You don't write the hooks. Alex does."
- "Your strategist already drafted next week."
- "Maya found the spike at 4am. You see it at 9."
- "It's Sunday. Jordan's plan is in your inbox."
- "Three hooks. One is the one. Alex tells you which."

## Hook patterns
- **The handoff:** "By the time you open the app, [agent] already [action]."
- **The org chart:** "I have a [role] now. Her name is [name]. She [does X]."
- **The contrast:** "ChatGPT gives you a draft. Sovexa gives you a team."
- **The morning ritual:** "Monday 7am. Trend report on the desk. I didn't write it."
- **The decision frame:** "I haven't typed a caption in [N] days. I just approve them."

## What every post must do
1. Make the reader feel like a CEO (or want to feel like one).
2. Imply the *org chart* — not the model.
3. Be specific. A real action by a real-named employee beats a generic claim.
